## 1. What Is DealtHands?

DealtHands puts you in the shoes of a young adult making real financial decisions. Over five rounds, you will choose a career, manage loans, pick a place to live, choose a vehicle, and navigate family life — all while unexpected events (Game Changers) can shake up your finances at any time.

At the end, you will see how your choices compare to your classmates on a leaderboard. The goal isn't just to "win" but to understand how small financial decisions compound into very different life outcomes.

---

## 2. Joining a Session

You do **not** need to create an account to play.

1. Open the application in a web browser (your educator will give you the link).
2. Click **Join Session** on the home page.
3. Enter your **name** and the **5-digit Join Code** your educator provides.
4. Click **Join**.

> **Keep this browser tab open** for the entire game. Closing it may disconnect you from the session.

---

## 3. The Waiting Lobby

After joining, you will see a waiting screen that confirms your name. Your educator controls when the game starts. Wait here until the game begins (your screen will update automatically).

---

## 4. How Rounds Work

There are **5 rounds**. Each round, your educator opens the round and you are given a **card** — a life decision with a financial consequence.

### Your Card

Each card describes a specific choice (e.g., *"You took a $15,000 car loan"*) and shows a monthly dollar amount. This amount will be applied to your financial running total.

### Submitting

When you are ready, click **Submit** to lock in your card. Once submitted, you cannot change your answer for that round.

Your educator controls when the round closes. If the round closes before you submit, your card is automatically submitted at its default amount.

---

## 5. Game Changers

At certain points in the game, a **Game Changer** will appear as a full-screen event before your card. Game Changers are random life events (either positive or negative) that affect your finances on top of your card choices.

Examples:
- *"You received a work bonus"* — your income increases
- *"Medical emergency"* — a one-time cost is deducted from your total
- *"You were laid off"* — your monthly income drops significantly

You must **dismiss the Game Changer screen** (click the button) before you can see your card for that round. The Game Changer effect is applied automatically.

Whether you get a Game Changer, and which one, depends on your previous decisions and the difficulty level your educator chose.

## 6. The Budget Calculator

A **Budget Calculator** sidebar is available during each round. It shows your current financial picture based on choices you have made so far:

| Metric | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Monthly Income** | Your take-home pay from your career choice. |
| **Monthly Expenses** | Sum of all your ongoing costs (loans, housing, transportation, etc.). |
| **Available** | Income minus expenses — what you have left over each month. |
| **Financial Health** | Healthy / Struggling / Critical, based on how much of your income is left over. |

The calculator also uses **Dave Ramsey's recommended budget percentages** to flag whether individual expenses are too high:
- Housing should be ≤ 25% of income
- Transportation should be ≤ 10% of income
- Food should be ≤ 15% of income
- Savings should be ≥ 10% of income

Use the calculator to guide your decisions — it's there to help, not to judge.

## 7. The Five Rounds

### Round 1 — Career

You choose your starting career and income level. This sets your **monthly income** for the rest of the game. All future expenses are measured against this number, so your career choice has the biggest impact on your overall financial health.

### Round 2 — Student Loans

You decide whether you carry student loan debt, and if so, how much. Student loan payments are calculated on a **10-year repayment plan at 4.5% interest** and will reduce your available monthly income for the remainder of the game.

### Round 3 — Transportation

You choose your vehicle situation. Options may include no car, a car purchased outright with cash, or a car financed with a loan. Car loan payments are calculated on a **5-year repayment plan at 5% interest**.

### Round 4 — Housing

You decide where you live. Options include renting alone, living with a roommate, or owning a home. Housing is typically your largest monthly expense.

### Round 5 — Family

You make decisions about marriage, children, and related lifestyle expenses. These affect your monthly costs and can also change which Game Changers you are eligible to receive.

---

## 8. End of Game

After all 5 rounds, the game moves to the **Results** screen.

### The Leaderboard

Students are ranked by their **cumulative financial score** — a running total of all their monthly amounts across all rounds. Higher scores indicate better financial outcomes.

Your **Financial Health** status — Healthy, Struggling, or Critical — reflects what percentage of your income you had left over at the end:
- **Healthy**: 30% or more available
- **Struggling**: 10–29% available
- **Critical**: Less than 10% available

### What This All Means

A high leaderboard score doesn't automatically mean you made the "right" choices for real life, and a low score doesn't mean you made bad ones. The debrief discussion with your educator is where the real learning happens. Think about:

- Which decisions had the biggest impact on your outcome?
- Did any Game Changers feel unfair? Could you have been better prepared for them?
- How do your choices reflect real behavioral economics concepts like loss aversion, present bias, or social pressure?
